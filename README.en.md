DUMANG is a registered mechanical keyboard brand owned by Shenzhen BeyondQ Technology Co., Ltd. The brand focuses on the design and manufacture of modular mechanical keyboards, combining a magnetic modular architecture, DIY-friendly switch and keycap compatibility, and rugged industrial design. The result is a "golden weapon" that players can freely expand and evolve over time.

## DK6 Modular Keyboard

The DK6 series is DUMANG's flagship line of modular mechanical keyboards. It uses a strong-magnetic attachment between the keyboard body and the swappable DKM1 key modules, supports Cherry MX-compatible switches and standard keycaps, and is built for high playability and long-term extensibility.

### Core Features

#### Modular Magnetic Architecture

- The DK6 main body and the DKM1 key modules are connected via strong magnets. Modules can be attached and detached without tools, allowing on-the-fly reconfiguration.
- The magnetic hold has been verified by a real-world stress test: four smartphones weighing approximately 667 g in total can be placed on a module without it shifting. Daily typing and transport will not loosen the connection.

#### DIY-Friendly DKM1 Key Module

- DKM1 supports both keycap and switch replacement, and is compatible with standard Cherry MX switches as well as most standard keycap profiles on the market.
- A wide range of optional accessories is available, letting players freely customize feel, sound, and appearance.
- Additional key modules, keycaps, and switches can be purchased separately to continuously strengthen and personalize your "golden weapon".

#### Rugged Industrial Design

- Designed for heavy-use gaming scenarios, emphasizing long-term stability and consistent typing feel.

### DIY Guide

#### Replacing Keycaps

- **Removal**: Pull the keycap straight up.
- **Installation**: Pay attention to the orientation of the keycap cross stem — the slot is wider in one axis than the other. **Do not rotate the keycap by 90°**, or it will jam or damage the switch and keycap.

#### Replacing Switches

- **Removal**: Use a switch puller (may need to be purchased separately) to grip the two side latches of the switch, then pull the switch straight up.
- **Installation**:
  1. First inspect the switch pins to make sure they are straight and vertical; do not install with bent pins.
  2. Align the pins with the corresponding holes and keep the switch perpendicular to the PCB.
  3. Apply even, gentle pressure straight down until the switch is fully seated.
- **Important**:
  - Never press the switch in at an angle — this can damage the switch, the hot-swap socket, or the PCB.
  - Never force the switch down. If it does not seat easily, realign and try again. When in doubt, remove the keycap first for better access.

### Usage & Care Tips

- Before replacing keycaps or switches, disconnect the keyboard from the host to prevent accidental input.
- A dedicated switch puller is recommended; avoid prying with screwdrivers or other improvised tools.
- Keep the magnetic contact surfaces clean. Metal shavings, oil, or dust can affect magnetic hold and electrical contact.
- Store spare modules separately when not in use, and avoid prolonged heavy loads on top of the keyboard.
